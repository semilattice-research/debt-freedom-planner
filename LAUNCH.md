# Launch Plan — Debt Freedom Planner

**Goal:** 100+ users. **Channel strategy (from Semilattice S3):** 52.4% of UK adults decide whether to try a money tool based on trusting the source, and word-of-mouth dominates (84.3% would share a useful tool). So: launch where trust already exists, write like a helpful person not a marketer, and let the built-in share button compound.

**Live URL:** https://semilattice-research.github.io/debt-freedom-planner/

## Channel order (highest trust-fit first)

1. **Reddit — r/UKPersonalFinance** (1.8m members). Strict no-self-promo rules: do NOT cold-post a link. Approach: answer debt-payoff questions genuinely; the weekly "mentions" thread or mod pre-approval for free non-commercial tools is the legitimate route. Honest framing: "I built a free calculator, no signup/ads/data collection, open source."
2. **Reddit — r/DebtFreeUK, r/povertyfinanceuk, r/UKFrugal** — smaller, more tolerant of genuinely free tools; same honest framing.
3. **MoneySavingExpert forum — Debt-Free Wannabe board.** The single most trusted UK money community (S3: MSE is the first port of call for 50.4%). Read board rules; introduce the tool in context.
4. **Hacker News — Show HN.** Not the target user, but devs share tools onward; the "no signup, no tracking, all client-side, open source" angle lands well there. Title: "Show HN: Free UK debt payoff calculator — no signup, all client-side".
5. **Product Hunt** — secondary; UK consumer reach is limited but it builds linkable credibility.

## Post drafts

### Show HN
> **Show HN: UK debt payoff calculator — free, no signup, everything stays in the browser**
> I used simulated-audience research (Semilattice) to pick what to build: UK adults' biggest complaint about money apps is "data, not advice", and 94% prefer tools that don't connect to their bank. So this is the opposite of a fintech app: a single static page. You type in your debts, it shows the cheapest payoff order (avalanche vs snowball), your debt-free date, and exactly what to pay this month. localStorage only, no analytics, no server. Feedback welcome — especially from anyone who's used it with real numbers.

### Reddit (free-tool-friendly subs)
> **I made a free debt payoff calculator for UK debts (no signup, no bank linking, nothing leaves your browser)**
> After reading one too many "which debt do I pay first?" threads, I built a small free tool: list your debts (cards, overdraft, loans, BNPL), say what you can afford monthly, and it shows the cheapest payoff order vs the "smallest first" method, your debt-free date, and what to pay on each debt this month. It's a static page — no account, no ads, no affiliate links, your numbers never leave your device. Not advice, just arithmetic — and if you're struggling, StepChange/National Debtline are linked prominently. Would genuinely appreciate feedback on what's confusing or missing.

## Measurement

No tracking on the site (by design — 85.5% abandon over data sharing). Proxy metrics:
- GitHub Pages traffic (Insights → Traffic: unique visitors, no user data)
- Upvotes/comments/shares on launch posts
- GitHub stars/issues

**Definition of "100 users": 100+ unique visitors in GitHub traffic stats with engagement evidence (comments/feedback), within 14 days of posting.**
